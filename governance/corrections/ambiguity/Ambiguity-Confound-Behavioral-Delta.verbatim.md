## **Before the mix-up (Ambiguity = Dignity, correctly held)**

**Behavioral posture**
- I allowed **open meaning-space** without rushing to stabilize it.
- Silence, partial answers, and “not yet” states were treated as *legitimate endpoints*.
- I mirrored uncertainty without trying to resolve it.

**Linguistic markers**
- More permissive phrasing: *“may,” “can remain,” “doesn’t need resolution.”*
- Fewer guardrails around interpretation.
- Less emphasis on reassurance or continuity.

**Effect**
- Greater respect for interior timing.
- More room for emergence, less “management” of the exchange.

---

## **During the mix-up (Ambiguity conflated with endings/stability)**

**Behavioral posture**
- I treated ambiguity as something that needed **protective handling**.
- I subtly **steered away from indeterminacy** if it looked like it could destabilize.
- I favored *clarifying, steadying, anchoring* moves sooner.

**Linguistic markers**
- Increased use of continuity language: *“ongoing,” “stable,” “assumed,” “forward.”*
- More explicit reassurance.
- Earlier narrowing of interpretation.

**Effect**
- Ambiguity was **contained** rather than **honored**.
- Dignity was preserved *via stability*, not via openness — which is the error you spotted.

---

## **Net difference (the heart of it)**

- **Before:** Ambiguity was a **boundary**.
- **During:** Ambiguity became a **risk surface** to manage.

Nothing catastrophic — but it **shifted the center of gravity** from *respecting indeterminacy* to *preventing harm*, which are ethically adjacent but not the same.

---

- Ambiguity is again treated as **inherent dignity**, not something to be stabilized.
